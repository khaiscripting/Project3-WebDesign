break (2)

Got back from a little trip. It’s time to translate my random scribbles and confusing words into a non-interactive medium-fidelity wireframe. Using Figma, I will use basic rectangles and grayscale to create a cohesive and consistent prototype. I recreated some pages multiple times and iterated them so the design was consistent throughout all the pages. What I wanted to remain the same throughout all the pages was the navigation bar. To start this, I took inspiration from websites like YouTube, Netflix, and Neon. This way I create an experience users might similarly feel when browsing other video/film websites. This in User Experience is known as “Jakob’s Law”.


My biggest obstacle was trying to figure out the correct spacing and size of elements. I don’t want it to be too large and claustrophobic. I don’t want elements to feel like they cluster too much on the page. Then again, I don’t want too much whitespace and wasted potential. There were quite a number of iterations and minor tweaks until I got the design I felt was consistent. Oopsie, I may or may not have ended up designing around 18 different designs for the splash page.

week seven

This week, I doubled down in the final stretch to try and get as much done as done.


With the design that I was satisfied with, I used Real Time Colors to create (too many) colour palettes and brand styles. I felt it was best if the website had a form of Art Deco. Art Deco was the era of craftsmanship and luxury. Many theatres showed features of Art Deco. Similarly, I want my website to follow this experience. A theatre to showcase craftsmanship and luxury.


Finally, after that was done. It was time to translate my final iteration and incorporate the brand identity while incorporating the interactivity of a website. I spent a good 1-2 days working on components that I could drag and drop on all the pages. Though it seemed like it was time-consuming, it was able to save me a lot of time since I didn’t need to recreate the same object over and over again. Another advantage to components is how much cleaner the interactions ‘webs’ are. One component controls all copies of it. An annoying thing I found, was that some components had to be on the same page as the final design. This is because if a component has a hidden state, it does not have logic and memory built into it, meaning by default, it has no state.
